# Design Engineer 001

**Role:** Freelance Design Engineer
**Difficulty:** Medium
**Time target:** 2-4 hours
**Status:** 🟢 Open

---

## Context

FunnelEnvy builds AI-powered agents that generate landing pages, experiment variations, and design assets for enterprise clients. The output is structurally sound but needs a human with real design taste to make it production-ready.

We're hiring a freelance design engineer who can take AI-generated output, refine it in Figma, and ship working code. This challenge tests exactly that workflow.

---

## The Brief

**Signalflow** is a fictional B2B SaaS company (pipeline intelligence for revenue teams). They need a landing page that converts VP Sales and RevOps leaders into free trial signups.

Our AI agent generated a first-pass landing page. It's functional but generic. Your job is to make it good.

### You receive:

- **[ai_wireframe.html](ai_wireframe.html)** - The AI-generated landing page. Open it in a browser. This is your starting point.
- **[BRAND_GUIDE.md](BRAND_GUIDE.md)** - Signalflow's brand system (colors, typography, spacing, component specs, voice).

### You submit:

Place all files in `submissions/firstname-lastname/` and open a PR.

1. **Figma file** - Your redesigned landing page as a polished, component-based Figma design. We want to see how you think about layout, hierarchy, spacing, and brand application. Use proper auto-layout, named components, and design tokens where appropriate. Include your Figma link (view access enabled) in the PR template.

2. **Working HTML/CSS** - Your implementation of that Figma design as a single HTML file. It should be responsive, use the brand system correctly, and look like something you'd ship to a client. No frameworks required - clean HTML/CSS/vanilla JS is fine.

3. **Rationale (max 1 page)** - What did you change and why? We're not looking for an essay. We want to see how you think about design decisions. What problems did you identify in the AI output? What tradeoffs did you make?

---

## What We're Evaluating

This is not a pixel-perfection test. We're evaluating:

- **Design judgment** - Can you identify what's wrong with AI output and fix it with real taste?
- **Brand application** - Can you take a brand guide and apply it with intention, not just copy hex codes?
- **Figma craft** - Are your files organized, componentized, and production-ready?
- **Design system fluency** - In practice, you'll be working within existing client Figma component libraries with established tokens, styles, and patterns. Your Figma file should demonstrate that you know how to build within constraints, not just from scratch. Show us componentization, design tokens, and structure that could slot into an existing system
- **Code quality** - Does your HTML/CSS reflect your design accurately? Is it clean, semantic, responsive?
- **Strategic thinking** - Does your rationale show you understand conversion, not just aesthetics?

Your submission will be blind-reviewed alongside a baseline generated by Claude using the same brief and inputs. If you outperform Claude, we want to talk.

---

## The Rules

- Use whatever tools you want. AI, plugins, component libraries, whatever.
- If you just paste this brief into an AI and submit its output, you'll get roughly the same answer we already have. That's a tie. **Ties don't advance.**
- To outperform, you need to add something AI can't: taste, judgment, creative decisions, conversion instinct.
- Don't overthink the copy. Minor copy edits are fine, but we're hiring a design engineer, not a copywriter.
